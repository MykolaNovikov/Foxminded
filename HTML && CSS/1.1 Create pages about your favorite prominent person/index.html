Taras Shevchenko

Taras Grigorovich Shevchenko is a ukrainian poet, novelist, thinker, painter, engraver, ethnographer, public figure.

(1814-1861)

years of life
"Fight - you will win, God helps you! For you is the truth, for you is the glory and the holy will!"

Shevchenko was an orphan and grew up in difficult conditions, working on peasant farms and in lordly domains. Thanks to his talent and perseverance in learning, Shevchenko gained an education and fame as an artist and poet. His works, in particular the collection "Kobzar", impressed readers with their depth and pathos. In his poems, he expressed patriotic feelings and sympathy for the poor and disenfranchised. His works are often seen as a symbol of the struggle for the rights and freedom of the Ukrainian people.

Shevchenko was also an active public figure, he opposed peasant serfdom and social inequality. His political activities led to the exile of Shevchenko to Siberia, where he spent more than 10 years. His legacy remains extremely important for Ukrainian national and cultural development, and he is still considered a symbol of the struggle for freedom, justice and dignity.
